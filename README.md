# Opus Civile

*The Work of Civilization*

---

Opus Civile is a worldbuilding project set in an alternate history where an 
industrial revolution takes root during the mid-to-late Roman Republic. It asks 
a simple but sprawling question: what happens when the machinery of industry 
arrives centuries too early, into a world that is not ready for it?

The Roman Republic — fractious, decentralized, alive with competing factions and 
ambitions — is not the tidy backdrop of an empire. There is no single authority 
to harness new technology, no emperor to decree its use or suppression. Instead, 
innovation cascades unevenly across the Republic: wealth accumulates in one city, 
an idea spreads to another, a faction gains an edge, and the world shifts a 
little more. The revolution is not an event. It is a tide.

This project explores that world — its politics, its people, its technology, and 
the profound social upheaval that follows when the old ways of doing things are 
no longer enough.

---

The worldbuilding developed here forms the foundation for a story, or perhaps 
several. The intent is to craft at least one standalone narrative set within this 
world — one that could stand on its own, but might grow into something larger if 
the world proves rich enough to hold it.

---

*This is a living project. The world comes first. The stories will follow.*
